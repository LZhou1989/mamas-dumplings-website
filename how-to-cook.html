Pan Fried Dumplings (Gyoza Style)

Crispy bottom with tender top - the perfect combination of textures!

1

Heat the Pan

Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add 1-2 tablespoons of oil (vegetable or sesame oil works best).

→
2

Arrange Dumplings

Place frozen dumplings in the pan, flat side down. Arrange them in a circle or rows, leaving space between each.

→
3

Add Water

Pour 1/3 cup of water mixed with half a teaspoon of flour into the pan (be careful of splatter). Immediately cover with a lid.

→
4

Steam and Cook

Let the dumplings steam for 8-10 minutes until the water has evaporated and the bottoms are golden brown.

→
5

Serve and Enjoy

Carefully remove from pan and serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce!

Boiled Dumplings (Traditional Style)

Tender and juicy dumplings cooked in gently simmering water.

1

Boil Water

Fill a large pot with water and bring to a rolling boil. Add a pinch of salt to the water.

→
2

Add Dumplings

Gently drop frozen dumplings into the boiling water. Stir gently to prevent sticking.

→
3

Simmer Gently

Reduce heat to medium and simmer for 6-8 minutes until dumplings float to the surface.

→
4

Drain and Serve

Use a slotted spoon to remove dumplings from water. Drain well and serve immediately.

Steamed Dumplings (Dim Sum Style)

Light and fluffy dumplings cooked with gentle steam heat.

1

Prepare Steamer

Fill a steamer pot with water and bring to a boil. Line steamer basket with parchment paper or cabbage leaves.

→
2

Arrange in Steamer

Place frozen dumplings in the steamer basket, leaving space between each to prevent sticking.

→
3

Steam to Perfection

Cover and steam for 8-10 minutes until dumplings are translucent and fully cooked.

→
4

Serve Hot

Carefully remove from steamer and serve immediately while hot and fluffy.
